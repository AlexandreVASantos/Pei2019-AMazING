Context

With the advancement of technology, it becomes even more important to be the first to present new systems and solutions. So, with that in mind, AMazING was created in 2010 to allow people inside IT (Instituto de Telecomunicações) to remotely connect to one or more nodes. This is a really good concept since it can help both students and researchers at the building and it's a way to connect work that shares the same principles and/or objectives. Since we all want to be the first it is important to have a system that allows to run our work and do tests at any possible time, if connected to the IT network.

Features

Since the main experiences made with nodes will be wireless, our system provides a menu to easily change configurations to free the user from the cognitive load adjacent to iw command. That means the user can change SSID, rate transmition, wireless channel, IP address, and many other things, with a click of a button.

Risks

Everyone living in Aveiro, a coast line city, knows that the weather conditions are nowhere near ideal. Even if the sun is out, it's no surprise when everything turns grey and you get absolutely flooded in a 5 minute rainstorm, or worse, you get a whole week of non-stop heavy rain and harsh winds. This means that the nodes on the rooftop of Instituto de Telecomunicações have to be protected from all the conditions mentioned earlier in order to make this system viable and lifelong. However some things need to be sacrificed in order to achieve maximum potencial from wireless antennas. In order to do that, we have to minimize the number of walls in their signal path, which makes them exposed and vulnerable to the weather conditions. Furthermore, with the possibility of the nodes being active all the time, there's the possibility of great temperature oscillation, so we added two fans inside the plastic box around the APU board to heat dissipation. If the plastic box and the wooden top can't protect what's inside, the plastic fans would collapse and wouldn't dissipate the heat, causing the failure of the APU board.
