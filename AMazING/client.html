Context

With the progress and growth in technology, it becomes even more important to be the first to present new systems and solutions. So, with that in mind, AMazING was created in 2010 as an outdoor test system to fulfill the needs of researchers of more realistic scenarios. Since it bases in remote connections it is a really good concept cebause it can help both students and researchers at the building as a way to close the gap between work that shares the same principles and/or objectives. So, since we all want to be the first, it is important to have a system like this, that allows to run our work and do tests at any possible time, if connected to the IT network. However, due to weather conditions, the old system got damaged and a new aproach was idealized. Reuse the infraestructure and add features to the old system, like two Web Apps, one called Node Configuration App to remotely configure wi-fi settings on nodes and other Switch controller App to enable and disable power remotely rather than connected via cable to the switch.

Features

The main experiences made with nodes will be wireless, so, our Node Configuration App provides a menu to easily change wi-fi configurations on the nodes to free the user from the cognitive load adjacent to the iw command. That means you can change SSID, rate transmission, wireless channel, IP address, and many other things, with the click of a button! Due to the physical infrastructure, the power supplied to the nodes will be provided via Poe (Power over Ethernet). So, the Switch Controller App allows the system manager, since it's not recommended that everyone has access to the central and vital equipment, to remotely enable or disable the switch Poe ports and consequently turning on and off nodes, rather than connected via cable to the console port of the switch in a 4 m2 room .

Risks

Everyone living in Aveiro, a coast line city, knows that the weather conditions are nowhere near ideal. Even if the sun is out, it's no surprise when everything turns grey and you get absolutely flooded in a 5 minute rainstorm, or worse, you get a whole week of non-stop heavy rain and harsh winds. This means that the nodes on the rooftop of Instituto de Telecomunicações have to be protected from all the conditions mentioned earlier in order to make this system viable and lifelong. However some things need to be sacrificed in order to achieve maximum potencial from wireless antennas. In order to do that, we have to minimize the number of walls in their signal path, which makes them exposed and vulnerable to the weather conditions. Furthermore, with the possibility of the nodes being active all the time, there's the risk of great temperature oscillation, so we added two fans inside the plastic box around the APU board for heat dissipation. If the plastic box and the wooden top can't protect what's inside, the plastic fans can collapse and fail to dissipate the heat, causing the failure of the APU board itself.
